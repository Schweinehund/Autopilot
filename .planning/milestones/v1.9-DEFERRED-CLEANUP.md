## WINDOWS-CLONE-DEEPNEST-TIMEOUT-01: Chain-Apex Cold-Clone Deep-Nesting Subprocess Cost on Windows (CARRIED FORWARD — WORSE at Phase 82)

**Scope:** Running the chain-apex from a cold fresh-clone on Windows exhibits an O(n²) cascade of cold Node subprocess spawns at the deepest nested guards, approaching the per-subprocess timeout and producing a spurious truncated FAIL. **Carried forward from v1.8; WORSE at Phase 82** because the apex chain is now `[48..81]` (+8 deeper than Phase 74's `[48..73]`).

**Evidence of correctness (not a regression):**
- The apex returns the SAME `26/10/1` on the warm main tree AND on Linux GHA (run `27966769510`).
- The cold-clone Windows behavior is a performance symptom, not a correctness defect.

**Mitigation adopted (Phase 82, per D-03):** The authoritative apex count is taken from the **warm main-tree** value cross-checked against the **Linux GHA apex job** (both `26/10/1`), NOT from a cold-clone apex re-run. The 9 non-apex validators are taken from fast standalone fresh-clone runs.

**Source:** v1.8 `WINDOWS-CLONE-DEEPNEST-TIMEOUT-01` (Plan 74-04) + `82-03-AUDIT-RESULTS.md` §Methodology Highlights (apex via warm-tree × Linux).

**Remediation options (v1.10+):** (1) cache-warm clone before the apex run; (2) raise the per-subprocess timeout for cold-clone re-audits; (3) cache chain-validator subprocess results within a single apex invocation to avoid redundant sub-spawns at depth ≥ 2 (the O(n²) mitigation — increasingly attractive as the chain deepens).

**Trigger-to-sweep (v1.10+):** The next terminal re-audit setup (or when chain depth affects warm-tree performance). Note: as the chain grows past `[48..81]`, option (3) becomes the durable fix.

**Estimated effort:** 30min (cache-warm) / 15min (timeout config) / 2-4h (O(n²) subprocess caching).

**Status:** CARRIED FORWARD from v1.8 (still unresolved; chain now deeper). DEFERRED to v1.10+. Does NOT affect harness correctness or cross-OS parity.
