    ## Step 0 — Verify Reseller Relationship

    Before creating a Zero-Touch portal account, confirm the reseller relationship is in place:

    - [ ] Devices were purchased from an [authorized Zero-Touch reseller](https://androidenterprisepartners.withgoogle.com/resellers/).
    - [ ] Reseller has confirmed the devices are registered in the Zero-Touch system.

    ## Create Zero-Touch Portal Account

    #### In Zero-Touch portal

    1. Navigate to [Zero-Touch portal](https://enterprise.google.com/android/zero-touch/customers).
       (Portal requires Google account login. An unauthenticated deep-link to customer-scoped pages is not possible.)
    2. Create a Google account at `accounts.google.com/signupwithoutgmail` using your corporate email (NOT a Gmail address).
       Sign in to the ZT portal with the new Google account.
    3. In the portal sidebar, locate these sections: **Configurations**, **Devices**, **Users**, **Resellers**, **Customer details**.

       > **What breaks if misconfigured:** Using a Gmail-based Google account rather than a corporate-email account ties the ZT portal account to a personal identity and risks account loss if the Gmail user leaves. Symptom appears in: ZT portal (account recovery blocked) and Intune admin center (linking fails on personal-account policy mismatch). Recovery: create a new account at `accounts.google.com/signupwithoutgmail` with corporate email; re-link. **Single-org-to-account-link constraint:** an org can only link to one Zero-Touch account; account unlinking while the org exists makes the account unrecoverable.

    **Network dependency** (per D-22): Devices need a working internet connection at first boot — Ethernet, Wi-Fi, or cellular. Captive-portal networks and networks without Google service access cause enrollment to fall through to consumer setup.

    ## Link Zero-Touch to Intune

    Two methods exist for linking the Zero-Touch portal to Intune. Choose based on your target enrollment mode.

    | Method | When to Use | Limitation |
    |---|---|---|
    | A: Via iframe in Intune admin center | COBO (Fully Managed) target only | Creates Fully Managed default configuration; do NOT use for Dedicated or COPE |
    | B: Direct Zero-Touch portal | Any mode (COBO, Dedicated, COPE) | More steps; full configuration control |

    > **Samsung admins:** Confirm KME is NOT also configured for the same device set before completing the ZT-Intune link. See [KME/ZT Mutual Exclusion](#kme-zt-mutual-exclusion) for details.

    (The inline callout above is the D-21 inline placement — required alongside the top-of-doc warning box.)

    ### Method A: Via iframe in Intune admin center

    #### In Intune admin center

    1. Sign in to [Intune admin center](https://endpoint.microsoft.com).
    2. Navigate to **Devices** > **By platform** > **Android** > **Device onboarding** > **Enrollment** > **Bulk enrollment methods** > **Zero-touch enrollment**.
    3. Ensure `update app sync` permission is granted first (**Tenant administration** > **Roles**).
    4. Sign in with the Google account provided to your reseller.
    5. Select the ZT account to link; select **Link**.

       > **What breaks if misconfigured:** Method A creates a default Fully Managed configuration that overrides the portal default. Using Method A for a Dedicated or COPE target results in devices enrolling as Fully Managed instead of the intended mode. Symptom appears in: Intune admin center (devices appear as Fully Managed) and device (wrong mode active). Recovery: use Method B (direct ZT portal) for non-COBO targets; delete the default configuration and create a mode-specific one.

    ## KME/ZT Mutual Exclusion (Samsung)
