MGP binding mechanics (verified HIGH — MS Learn `connect-managed-google-play` 2026-04-16):
1. Sign in to Intune admin center: `https://endpoint.microsoft.com` (or `go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2109431` per MS Learn)
2. Navigate: Devices > Enrollment > Android tab > Prerequisites > Managed Google Play
3. Accept data-sharing terms (Microsoft sends user/device info to Google)
4. Select "Launch Google to connect now"
5. Confirm prefilled Microsoft Entra account (Entra preferred since August 2024)
6. Follow Google prompts to create Admin account
7. Select "Allow and create account" to grant Intune permission
8. Four apps auto-approved in MGP store:
   - Microsoft Intune (`com.microsoft.intune`)
   - Microsoft Authenticator (`com.azure.authenticator`)
   - Intune Company Portal (`com.microsoft.windowsintune.companyportal`)
   - Managed Home Screen (`com.microsoft.launcher.enterprise`)

    ## Disconnect Consequences

    (Per D-16 — this is the highest-blast-radius failure mode. Dedicated section + inline callout at the binding step [see Step 1 and the Binding disconnected row in What Breaks above] ensures admins cannot miss this.)

    Disconnecting the Managed Google Play binding disables Android Enterprise device management for the entire tenant. **Required step sequence before disconnecting:**

    1. Retire all Android Enterprise personally-owned Work Profile devices.
    2. Retire all Android Enterprise corporate-owned Work Profile devices.
    3. Retire all Android Enterprise fully managed devices.
    4. Retire all Android Enterprise dedicated devices.
    5. Only then disconnect the binding.

    After disconnect, app assignments, OEMConfig assignments, and LOB app availability are lost. Re-binding creates a new enterprise relationship with Google — every app must be re-approved and every assignment must be recreated manually.
