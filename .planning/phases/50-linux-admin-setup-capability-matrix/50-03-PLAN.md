```markdown
## What is Linux Intune Enrollment?

[Plain-language explanation: 2-3 short paragraphs. Why your IT department asked you to do this; what gets installed; what changes about your laptop; what stays the same. Compare gently to "What is BYOD Work Profile?" structure in the Android analog. Bold for UI element names like **Microsoft Intune Portal** and **Microsoft Edge**.]

## Before you start

[6-item checklist, plain language. Bold for item titles.]

- **Ubuntu 22.04 LTS or 24.04 LTS** — older Ubuntu (20.04 and earlier) is no longer supported by Intune.
- **Microsoft Edge for Linux 102.x or higher** — needed for sign-in. Install from [microsoft.com/edge](https://www.microsoft.com/edge) if not already on your machine.
- **Your organization email and password** — same credentials you use for Outlook, Teams, etc.
- **A stable internet connection** — sign-in needs to reach Microsoft and your organization's identity service.
- **Administrator (sudo) access on your laptop** — needed once during install of the Intune Portal app.
- **Time:** about 15-20 minutes the first time.

## Enroll your device

[5 numbered steps for LIN-06. Plain language; bold for UI element names.]

1. **Install Microsoft Edge** (if not already installed). Download from [microsoft.com/edge](https://www.microsoft.com/edge), choose the deb (.deb) installer for Ubuntu, and install with `sudo apt install ./<downloaded-edge-deb>` in a terminal.

2. **Install the Intune Portal app.** Open a terminal and run the commands your IT department gave you (these add the Microsoft package source to apt and install `intune-portal`). If you do not have those commands, ask IT — they are documented in the admin guide your IT lead follows.

3. **Open the Intune Portal app and sign in.** Search for **Microsoft Intune Portal** in your applications. Click **Sign in** and enter your organization email and password. If your organization uses multifactor authentication, complete the prompt (e.g., approve in the Authenticator app).

4. **Complete compliance remediation if prompted.** The Intune Portal app may show a list of items your laptop needs to fix to be compliant — for example, "Set a password meeting policy" or "Enable disk encryption". Follow each on-screen instruction and click **Re-check** when done. Once everything is green, your device is enrolled and reporting compliant.

5. **Sign into Microsoft Edge with your organization account.** Open **Microsoft Edge**. Click your profile (top right) and choose **Sign in to sync data**. Enter your organization email and password. Once signed in, you can access work resources like Outlook web, SharePoint, and Teams web through Edge.

## Verify enrollment

[Mirror Android's enrollment-confirmation section. What the user sees when enrollment succeeds.]

- The Intune Portal app shows your device with a green check or "Compliant" status.
- In your Intune Portal, your IT department can see your device listed.
- Microsoft Edge can sign into Outlook web, Teams web, etc., without "blocked by policy" messages.

## Get help

[Mirror Android's "If something goes wrong" + "For IT helpdesk agents" sections.]

If something goes wrong:

- **"Sign-in failed":** Double-check your organization email and password. If multifactor authentication is required, approve the prompt promptly. If the issue persists, contact IT.
- **"Your device is not compliant":** The Intune Portal app shows what to fix — follow each instruction. If you cannot fix something (e.g., disk encryption is not available on your hardware), contact IT.
- **"Edge says I'm blocked":** Make sure you signed into Edge with your organization account in Step 5. If still blocked, your IT department's Conditional Access policy may scope to a specific Edge version — verify with `microsoft-edge --version` (must be 102 or higher).

For IT helpdesk agents:
- The L1 enrollment-failed runbook is at `docs/l1-runbooks/30-linux-enrollment-failed.md` (when published in Phase 51).
- The admin enrollment-config guide is at [admin-setup-linux/02-enrollment-profile.md](../admin-setup-linux/02-enrollment-profile.md).
```
